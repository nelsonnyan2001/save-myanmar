What has been happening?




1st February 2021

↓

4:00 a.m.

The democratically elected party leader and state counsellor Aung San Suu Kyi gets detained.

One by one, government officials and politicians follow.

6:30 a.m.

"The phone lines have been cut."

"Wi-fi and mobile data will probably follow soon."

Talk of a coup had been going on for several days now. Realizing our fears had been affirmed, most of the population set out to buy rations for the upcoming weeks.

8:00 a.m. - 3 p.m.

For a couple hours, the entire country started lost access to the internet.

It lasted 2 or 3 hours for some people, and over half a day for others.

Most TV channels no longer work. The ones that work are all military-owned.

3 p.m.

Unrest begins.

People take to Facebook, Instagram, Twitter and Reddit. We bring what we have to see what we can do.

We receive news that the military rule will go on for a year.

8 p.m.

For most of us, the realization has set in.

The realization things probably will not go back to the things they were. Not for a while

The realization that we cannot let history repeat.

We go to sleep amid rumors of all network connections being gone tomorrow.

2nd February 2021

↓

6:00 a.m.

Thankfully, our network connections haven't been cut off.

Work returns to normal for some of us. Others reach out to friends and family, checking in and making sure everything is fine.

Yet others continue the fight on social media.

9:00 a.m.

CIVIL DISOBEDIENCE

We will not protest on the streets. We will not provide an excuse for an actual military takeover.

Instead, we will protest the safest way we know how - everyday business will NOT resume until the detained military members are released.

3:00 p.m.

"Flights from Myanmar International Airport will be closed until 30th May 2021."

To some of us, this means nothing. To others, it is the end of any hopes we had of studying, working or meeting people we love abroad.

There are plans for the country to rally behind a peaceful protest at 8 o'clock tonight.

8:00 p.m.

The fateful banging of the pots and pans.

There's a belief in Myanmar, where we'll bang pots and pans, honk cars and cause a lot of nuisance in general.

We believe it drives away evil.

We hope it drives away evil.

We hope.

3rd February 2021

↓

7:00 a.m.

Day 3.

Day 3 of hoping something changes.

Day 3 of being worried about repeating history.

Day 3 of a military rule.

9:00 a.m.

It's funny, reading comments online. Governments express their "condemnations". Celebrities express their "concern".

At the same time, there's equally as many people criticizing the country. Criticizing our leaders, speaking as if they knew better than the people that live here.

A ping-pong match between people that don't live here, that have never experienced the horrors of absolute military rule.

11:00 a.m.

Doctors have carried the torch and ran with the flame of civil disobedience.

We hope as many people as possible will follow suit, soon.

Fake news runs amok among us, with a new rumor whispering fear in our ears every other hour.

6:00 p.m.

You're not allowed to detain a person for longer than 72 hours with no legal precedence.

Guess what?

Daw Aung San Suu Kyi gets arrested for possession of an unlicensed walkie talkie.

8:00 p.m.

The sound of pots and pans being banged together by a tired population rings throughout the streets.

We sing a song in defiance. Called "Kabar Ma Kyay", it's a play on words on the national anthem.

The noise goes on for noticeably longer than yesterday.

4th February 2021

↓

9:00 a.m.

Facebook is gone. Instagram is gone. Whatsapp is gone.

People look for workarounds. VPN companies are having a field day.

Oh, who will hear our cries now?

11:00 a.m.

More doctors join in the Civil Disobedience Movement.

Rumours of violence and danger of staying out past the curfew start leaking. No one is sure if this is real - whether people are just being fearmongers or if the days ahead are to be filled with bloodshed.

People unite under a theme of red, under unity never breaking the spirit of true democracy in Myanmar.

3:00 p.m.

There are peaceful protests in Mandalay. We wonder how long they will be peaceful for.

More fake news, more propaganda leaks as people start wondering if half the things they see online are real. Some of the leaks seem too gruesome to be, but these are gruesome times we are living in.

6:00 p.m.

As the days drag on to more grim news, we share stories of the bloodshed that happened some 30-odd years ago.

Lest they repeat.

8:00 p.m.

Punctual as ever. The drums of peace sound the streets of Myanmar.

The sound goes on for almost half an hour. People drive around in groups, honking horns where the houses are too far apart to noticeably beat their drums.

We hope tomorrow will bring a brighter future. We hope we will not have to beat these pans for much longer.
